empath: extracting smarter sentiments for antidepressants

Starting antidepressants can be scary, both from symptoms and from side effects. This can be all the worse in the first few weeks, particularly if you need to try several different medications before finding one that works.

It can be easy to feel alone when dealing with depression. But others are going through the same as you - understanding what they're feeling and how they're reacting can be a powerful thing.

By extracting conversations on antidepressants from social users on reddit.com, this app shares sentiments and understanding on antidepressant usage based on keywords for characterizing conversation topics, analysis of positive & negative sentiments expressed in comments, and even recommendations for likely successful alternate medications.

methods

The app implements three major components: keyword conversation characterization, sentiment analysis, and the recommender system.

Keyword sets are generated based on filtering via term frequency-inverse document frequency (TF-IDF). TF-IDF scoring is based on how frequently a word (more properly, a lemma, or word processed down to its base, without any attached grammatical information) appears in the target text (the term frequency). This score is then normalized by how frequently a word appears in similar texts, to account for words that are common across the board in related documents (and therefore aren't useful to characterize a particular text). This app scores the TF against an IDF calculated on unrelated web comments - thus, finding words that are common in comments discussing antidepressants, and uncommon in other web comments.

Sentiment scores are calculated using the recursive neural net (RNN) method implemented in the Stanford CoreNLP toolkit. This allows the sentiment analyzer to account for semantic relationships between words, rather than simply tallying the sentiment score based on single-word occurrences. The RNN method operates on parsed semantic structures mapping sentences into trees - this also allows sentiment scoring to be applied to subsections of sentences talking about particular drugs, as the sentiment score is recursively applied to every node in the tree.

The recommender system is based on simple logic rules, tracking the new medications mentioned subsequently by users of the input drug (assuming conversations are laid out chronologically). This is then filtered by a check that the expressed sentiment improved with the new drug, indicating that the switch was positive for that user.

The app reports these in a clean, visually-intuitive manner, showing the sentiment score for the chosen drug compared to the database average, a word bubbler for conversation topics, a switch recommendation, and a sample of processed reddit comments (ranked by score normalized to subreddit population to account for different topical forum sizes).
